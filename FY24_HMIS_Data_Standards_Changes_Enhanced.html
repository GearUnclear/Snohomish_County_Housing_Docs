FY24 HMIS Data Standards Changes

Comprehensive Analysis of Updates to the HMIS Data Standards Manual

Previous Version: FY2024 Manual → Current Version: FY2025 Manual

Total Changes Identified: 10 sections

Summary of Changes

  • Change #1: Version History Update
    Added FY2025 V1.7 revision notes to the version history table. The new version documents compliance updates made in April 2025 to align with the current Administration's Executive Orders. Affected sections include Person Centered Approaches to HMIS Data Collection, Emergency Shelter project information, and Universal Data Elements for Name (3.01), Social Security Number (3.02), and Race and Ethnicity (3.04). Additionally clarifies that the "additional specificity" field for race/ethnicity details is optional.
  • Change #2: Executive Order Compliance Details
    Detailed additions explaining the retirement of Gender (3.06) and Sexual Orientation (R3) data elements, plus updates to HP Targeting Criteria (V7) to comply with current Administration's Executive Orders. These elements are scheduled for software implementation during FY2026 HMIS Data Standards rollout.
  • Change #3: Equity Language Removal
    Removed extensive section discussing systemic racism, discrimination, and the overrepresentation of Black, Indigenous, and People of Color in homelessness systems. Also removed guidance on equity-focused data collection approaches, trauma-informed practices, and cultural humility requirements.
  • Change #4: Fair Housing Act Reference Removal
    Removed advisory paragraph about Fair Housing Act compliance that prohibited discrimination based on familial status and reminded projects they cannot deny admission to households with children except where permitted by federal program statute.
  • Change #5: SSN Collection Guidance Simplified
    Simplified Social Security Number collection instructions by removing detailed federal statute references (5 U.S.C. Section 522a) and specific prohibitions against denying services to clients who refuse to provide or don't know their SSN. Replaced with simpler language about clients who may not have access to their SSN.
  • Change #6: Gender Data Element Retired
    Complete removal of the Gender (3.06) data element section, which included detailed instructions for collecting self-reported gender identity, multiple response options (Woman, Man, Culturally Specific Identity, Transgender, Non-Binary, Questioning, Different Identity), and extensive guidance on client-centered approaches to gender data collection.
  • Change #7: Minor Formatting Correction
    Minor formatting correction: Fixed spacing in 'funded by BCP grant' text within the RHY Youth Eligibility section.
  • Change #8: Sexual Orientation Element Retired
    Complete removal of Sexual Orientation (R3) data element section, which was previously required for RHY, YHDP, and certain CoC programs. The subsequent data element R4 Last Grade Completed now appears in its place in the numbering sequence.
  • Change #9: Minor Formatting Correction
    Minor formatting correction: Fixed spacing in 'In the last three months' text within the trafficking and commercial sexual exploitation questions.
  • Change #10: HP Targeting Criteria Update
    Updated HP Targeting Criteria language from 'age/gender mix' to 'demographic mix' for household size requirements. Also replaced specific mention of 'overrepresented populations' with more general language about 'other criteria for targeting prevention determined by the CoC'.

Collection Point Record creation
Record the self-reported gender of each client served. When enrolling a client who already has
a record in the HMIS, verify that gender information is complete and accurately reflects how the
client identifies, and correct if it does not. Gender identity is a person’s internal perception of
themselves and may not match the sex they were assigned at birth. This element records one’s
gender identity and not sex assigned at birth.
HMIS users and data entry staff should apply a Client-Centered Approach to Recognizing
Gender Identities in Data Collection. Staff observations should never be used to collect
information on gender. Provide all options to every client. Even if staff think they can guess a
client's gender, every client must be asked for their self-reported information. If they prefer not to
provide it or say they don't know, do not select any response other than “Client doesn’t know” or
“Client prefers not to answer” on the client’s behalf. Gender does not have to match legal
documents and clients may not be asked about medical history or other information to try to
determine the person's gender. Simply asking, “Which of these genders best describes how you
identify?” is appropriate and focuses on the person's own internal knowledge of their gender.
If a client does not understand what a particular gender response means, the descriptions below
can be provided. Clients may select as many of the seven responses to ‘Gender’ as they would
like to for their preferred identity, need, or situation. However, a response of “Client doesn't
know” should not be used interchangeably with the response option “Questioning.”
“Questioning” is about exploring one’s gender identity. “Client doesn't know” should only be
selected when a client does not know their gender from the options available, including
“Questioning.” “Client doesn’t know”, “Client prefers not to answer”, and “Data not collected” are
not valid in conjunction with any other response.
If a client discloses having a culturally specific identity (e.g., Two-Spirit), transgender, non-
binary, questioning, or a different identity, staff should ask if the client prefers to have the HMIS
record reflect the client’s gender identity. For example, the availability of these options is not
intended to indicate that transgender individuals are expected to disclose their status; each
response is provided as an option in case an option (or more than one option) is better suited to
a client’s identity, needs, or situation. For instance, if a client identifies as a transgender man but
they do not want their transgender identity recorded in the HMIS, the staff person would select
“Man (Boy, if child)” instead of both “Man (Boy, if child)” and “Transgender”.
Clients may report different gender identities or present different gender expressions at different
projects within the same CoC. This may be because their gender identity has changed or
because they experience a different degree of safety at different projects. If staff are working
with a client who reports a gender identity that differs from the existing HMIS record, staff should
ensure that the client understands and is comfortable with their information being updated
across all projects prior to making any changes. Clients decide to which projects they will
disclose potentially sensitive information. Project staff should enter the self-reported information
as directed by the client.
